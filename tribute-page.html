Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

"Missile Man of India" & Former President

About

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931â€“2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the 'Missile Man of India' for his work in ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, he played a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

Timeline

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1960: Graduated in Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1980: Played a key role in India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1998: Led Pokhran-II nuclear tests.
  • 2002: Became the 11th President of India.
  • 2015: Passed away delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.

Legacy

Dr. Kalam inspired millions through his vision for a developed India, advocacy for education, and his simple, humble lifestyle. His books, such as Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds, continue to inspire generations.
